Thai-Curry

Description

Thai Curry: A Burst of Exotic Flavors

Dive into the vibrant world of Thai cuisine with this aromatic and creamy curry. A harmonious blend of rich coconut milk, spicy curry paste, tender chicken (or tofu), and crisp vegetables, this dish is a perfect balance of sweet, savory, and spicy. Serve it with steamed rice for a satisfying meal that brings the taste of Thailand to your table.

Ingredients

  • 400g chicken breast (or tofu for a vegetarian version), cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons red or green curry paste
  • 400ml coconut milk
  • 200ml chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 medium zucchini, sliced
  • 100g green beans, trimmed
  • 2 teaspoons fish sauce (or soy sauce for a vegetarian option)
  • 1 teaspoon brown sugar
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh basil or cilantro leaves, for garnish
  • Steamed rice, for serving

Steps

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the onion and sauté until translucent (about 3-4 minutes).

Step 2: Cook the Curry Base

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat.
  2. Add the onion and sauté until soft (about 2-3 minutes).
  3. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the curry paste and cook for another minute to release its aroma.

Step 3: Add the Liquids and Protein

  1. Slowly pour in the coconut milk, stirring to combine with the curry paste.
  2. Add the chicken stock and stir well.
  3. Add the chicken pieces (or tofu) and let them cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until almost cooked through.

Step 4: Add the Vegetables

  1. Add the bell peppers, zucchini, and green beans to the pan.
  2. Simmer for another 5-7 minutes, until the vegetables are tender but still slightly crisp.

Step 5: Season the Curry

  1. Stir in the fish sauce, brown sugar, and lime juice. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  2. Simmer for an additional 2 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
